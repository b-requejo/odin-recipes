Red Beans and Rice - Cajun Style

Hearty dish that has a kick

This is an authentic Louisiana red beans and rice. Very budget-friendly, but packed with flavor.

Ingredients

Serving size: 8 servings

  • 1 lb dry kidney beans
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 1 large green bell pepper, chopped
  • 2 stalks celery, chopped
  • 2 tbsp minced garlic
  • 6 cups water
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tbsp dried parsley
  • 1 tsp thyme
  • 1 tsp cajun seasoning (I use Tony Chacheres Creole Seasoning )
  • 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper
  • 1/4 tsp dried sage
  • 1 lb andouille sausage - sliced
  • 4 cups of water
  • 2 cups of long grained rice

Directions

  1. Soak the dry red beans overnight. Cover it with water and soak for at least 6 hours. For best results, soak them overnight.
  2. In a skillet, heat oil. Add the onions, bell peppersm celery abd garlic. Should take about 3-4 min.
  3. Rinse the beans that were soaking , and transfer to a large pot with about 6 cups of water.
  4. Add the cooked vegetables in the pot with the beans. Season with bay leaves, parsley, thyme, cayenne pepper, sage and the cajun seasoning. Bring this to a boil and reduce heat to medium - low and let simmer. Simmer for about 2 1/2 hours.
  5. After it has simmered for about 2 1/2 hours, add the cut up sausage to the beans and again simmer for 30 min.
  6. While that is simmering, in another saucepan, add water and rice. Once it is boiling, reduce the heat to a simmer. Simmer for about 20 min or until rice is tender and fluffy.
  7. Serve the beans over steamed white rice.
  8. Enjoy!
